My Story

Welcome, gorgeous. I am so glad your path has led you here to intersect with mine. Do you realize how much choice you get in the matter of your mental, emotional, and physical health? Have you found your glow? Allow me to share with you the parts of myself that fuel my passion to empower (and spread the glow to) fellow women who are on their own paths through motherhood.

In my mind, the most basic foundation of health is the principle that what we put into our bodies plays a significant role in our health.

For me, this foundation was set by growing up on a fairly self-sufficient organic farm in Iowa with parents who read every ingredient label. As a child, it was incredibly frustrating that I couldn’t just have the Doritos. But, I look back on this and am filled with gratitude towards my parents for laying this foundation.

Fast forward to high school, and I’m the overweight, shy girl who just wanted to look like the cover girls. So, following the advice of the cover girls, I forced myself to like celery (among other health foods) and I forced myself to run. The weight melted off, and I had built another story on top of my foundation of knowledge: exercise is good (check!).

Then, I started college at Iowa State University. I declared a kinesiology major. I was taught by some of the leading pioneers in exercise research. My foundation was starting to grow into a tower of knowledge about the body’s responses to exercise, disease, and nutrition. In my personal life, I worked out intermittently, taught group fitness classes, walked to class...yet, I found myself overweight with the worst acne I had ever had in my life. Again, I decided to make a change. However, this time around I had more knowledge about my body’s response to lifestyle factors. I switched my snacks to veggies, I disciplined myself to hold a regular workout routine, I studied better than I ever had in my four years of college, I honored my need for sleep and went to bed before my roommates. I also had so much fun with friends. My social life was flourishing.

This was the MOST JOY I had experienced since I couldn’t even remember when. And this all felt effortless.

My training with the Institute for Integrative Nutrition teaches that during that point in my life, I was nourishing myself with Primary Foods through rich relationships, a deep spiritual practice, enjoyable regular exercise, and a fulfilling career (I was loving my class load that semester!) The actual food that I ate became secondary to these other factors that fueled my passion for day-to-day life.

Now fast forward……

Two and a half years later I found out I was going to be a mom! I consumed endless knowledge about how to optimize my baby’s development during each growth period in the womb. I kept up with my daily What to Expect app to monitor developmental milestones. I tried to stay as active as I could through that winter.

Then something interesting happened: I ended up noticing that many newer moms in my life were having a lot more complaints about their aching bodies, or inability to sleep, or a prenatal complication that their doctor remedied with lifestyle changes. This seriously made me question, “Why am I having such an easy pregnancy when these other mamas are having such a rough go at it?” Being a science-minded person, I started to dig deeper when friends would bring up their latest pregnancy pain. And every conversation ended the same: these women would self-prescribe what they knew they needed. Whether it be more water, more activity, more sleep, less sugar, less processed foods, less stress…it was almost always lifestyle related.

This current lifestyle I choose has been a journey that’s intertwined science and real life experience. It has been a journey of discovering what my mind and body need, and realizing just how much control I actually hold over my own well-being. And why should pregnancy be any different? Yes, there are factors that we just have no control over, such as genetics, hormone changes, and a body that is being pushed to the max. Mama, you DO get a say in the matter of how you feel. You should be demanding only the best for your mind, body, and growing babe. Motherhood requires 100% giving and oftentimes we don’t take. It’s time to fill your cup too. As a mama who has found her light through a sustainable, happy and healthy life, I want to share this knowledge and experience with every other mama. You can actualize a pregnancy and a life of mothering that wakes you up with glowing energy. And soon this radiance will become contagious, and spread to another mama who is in pursuit of her glow too.

All those other things, they are the glass that contains the lamp, but you are the light inside.” Cassandra Clare
